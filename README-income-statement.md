# Income Statement
In order to analyse income statement, we need to know the parameters first. Let's separate out each parameter from each other to get better understanding.

## Revenue
1. Operations: The income generated from a company's core business activities such as selling products, or services.
2. Non-operating/Others: The income generated from activities not related to principal business like interest income, gains from asset sales, etc.

## Expense
1. General: The cost essential for running a business such as rents, utilities, office supplies, paying for staff, etc.
2. Total Operating Expense: The total cost including general operations cost and the cost spent on marketing and shipping of product including professional cost.

## EBITDA(Earning Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation and Amortization)
EBITDA is the net earning which a company recieves, separating total operating expenses from total revenue however, the condition is excluding of interest, tax, depreciation and amortization.

$$ Depreciation / Amortization = EBITDA(76) - EBIT(68) $$

## EBIT(Earning Before Interest and Taxes)
EBIT is the net earning which a company recieves, separating total operating expenses from total revenue however, the condition is excluding of interest and tax.

$$ EBIT = Total Revenue(258) - Total Operating Expense(190) $$

## Profit Without Tax
Profit Without Tax is the net earning which a company recieves, separating total operating expenses from total revenue however, the condition is excluding of tax.

$$ Interest(Payment to bank/lenders) = EBIT(68) - Profit without tax(58) $$

## Net Profit
Net Profit is the net earning which a company recieves, separating total operating expenses from total revenue.

$$ Tax(Payment to Government) = Profit without tax(58) - Net Profit(42) $$
